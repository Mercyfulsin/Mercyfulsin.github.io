About Me

My name is Andres Ruiz-Velediaz. I am, as of writing this page, 25 years old and am currently taking part of a bootcamp for Web Development. I have always been fascinated by computers and programming but have never had the support, friends or financial stability to really get into it at a young age. It all began when my soccer coach asked if I wanted to be a part of a youth program to get kids into computers. I accepted and quickly fell in love with everything. The coach saw my passion and decided to pay for my first year in the program. I would continue to go and be a part of the wonderful, yet very small, program from 5th grade till the end of 9th. The program didn't have enough funding to continue teaching my class and was cancelled. By then I had done a great varying of things, from Dreamweaver, HTML, Building a Computer, Flash and Photoshop.

Being a young boy, I was obviously sucked into the gaming aspect of computers. I then became fascinated with the idea of "hacking," as most people call it. I wanted to be able to beat a game using cheats to make my life easier. I quickly scattered down the rabbit hole and made lots of online friends and ended up being a part of many "great" projects and communities. I say "great" because it seemed like a lot to me, but in reality, the communities couldn't have exceeded 5,000 people. I've made my own personal "hacks" and released them for public usage on different forums for different games. Unfortunately no one in real life that I knew of understood anything to do with computers. I was alone and could only find solice in the small amount of friends I made online.

My parents would berate me every night about how I would be staying up for hours on end without food or going out. Constantly telling me I was wasting my time and that all of this would amount to nothing. Being the young impresionable kid that I was, I quickly became depressed and unmotivated. I lost any and all reason to continue studying / practicing code. Years passed and the people around me continued to call me a genius with computers and depend on me. But they didn't realize that once you join this field, you quickly realize how LITTLE you actually know. I always tell them that I am a COMPLETE beginner and that there is an infinite amount of things I don't know. Since then I have attempted to get back into computers and keeping up with news on the latest tech. Yet now I feel completely lost. Almost as if the six or more years of neglecting computers had made me completely stupid and unable to learn. This is when I decided I needed some kind of strong-armed attempt at getting back into the scene. So I joined the UCSD Extension Web Development Bootcamp in order to focus me by providing physical attendence and teachers for guidence. I hope that by the end of the course I will be back to the way I used to be, and take initiative to learn anything and everything.
